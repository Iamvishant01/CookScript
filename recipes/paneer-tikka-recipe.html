Paneer Tikka Recipe

Ingredients:
For the Marinade:

250g paneer (cottage cheese), cut into cubes
1/2 cup thick yogurt (preferably Greek yogurt)
1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 tbsp red chili powder
1 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp garam masala
1 tbsp coriander powder
1 tbsp cumin powder
Salt to taste
1 tbsp mustard oil (optional, for authentic taste)
1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
1 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro (optional)
For the Tikka:

1 onion, sliced into rings
1 bell pepper, sliced into rings
Fresh coriander or mint leaves (for garnish)
Lemon wedges
Instructions:
Prepare the Marinade:

In a mixing bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, salt, and mustard oil. Mix well until smooth.
Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and coat them evenly. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (for better flavor, marinate for 2-3 hours).
Prepare the Skewers:

While the paneer is marinating, prepare the vegetables (onion and bell pepper) by slicing them into rings.
After marinating, thread the paneer cubes onto skewers, alternating with onion and bell pepper rings.
Cook the Paneer Tikka:

Option 1 (Grilling): Preheat a grill or oven to medium-high heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally until the paneer is slightly charred and cooked through. You can brush the paneer with a little oil or butter during grilling to keep it moist.
Option 2 (Stovetop): Heat a non-stick pan or grill pan over medium heat. Place the skewers on the pan and cook the paneer for 3-4 minutes on each side, turning until golden brown and slightly charred.
Serve:

Once the paneer is cooked, remove it from the skewers and place it on a serving plate.
Garnish with fresh coriander or mint leaves, lemon wedges, and serve with a side of green chutney or yogurt dip.
Enjoy your Paneer Tikka, a flavorful Indian appetizer that is both spicy and smoky!
